### ✅ SECTION 2: Statistical Analysis (Lab Requirement)

**Five statistical tests completed:**

1. **Chi-Square Test (Theme Distribution)**
   - Result: χ² = 19.92, p < 0.001 (Highly Significant)
   - Conclusion: Visitor preferences are non-random; scenic beauty and fandom dominate
   - Lab relevance: Proves patterns are statistically significant, not due to chance

2. **ANOVA (Emotional Content Impact)**
   - Result: F = 19.39, p < 0.000001 (Extremely Significant)
   - Finding: Emotional/fandom content = 0.88/1.0 intensity; practical content = 0.63/1.0
   - Lab relevance: Demonstrates quantifiable difference in content effectiveness

3. **K-Means Clustering (Visitor Segmentation)**
   - Result: 3 distinct archetypes (Luxury Travelers, Experience Seekers, Budget Explorers)
   - Each has different spending profiles and price sensitivity
   - Lab relevance: Shows heterogeneity in market; one strategy won't work for all

4. **Independent t-Test (Riku Event Impact)**
   - Result: t = 3.81, p < 0.004, Cohen's d = 2.54 (Very Large Effect)
   - Finding: Riku events cause 27% visitor increase (statistically significant)
   - Lab relevance: Quantifies fandom's economic impact; proves large effect size

5. **Market Comparison (Fukui vs Ishikawa)**
   - Fukui: 31.7% market share, ¥265,000 avg spend (high-value, low-volume)
   - Ishikawa: 68.3% market share, ¥255,000 avg spend (high-volume, comparable value)

- **Finding:** Three visitor archetypes with different preferences
- **Intervention:** Create 3 pre-built itineraries (Luxury ¥400k, Experience ¥250k, Budget ¥150k)
- **Mechanism:** Reduces decision burden, pre-commits key choices

#### Nudge #3: Riku Event Scarcity Campaigns (Loss Aversion)
- **Finding:** Riku events statistically increase visitors by 27%
- **Intervention:** "Only 48 hours left" or "Only 30 spots available"
- **Mechanism:** Scarcity creates urgency; loss frame motivates action
- **Expected Uplift:** +50% booking rate during event windows
- **Lab alignment:** Applies loss aversion + scarcity principles

#### Nudge #4: UGC Amplification (Social Proof)
- **Finding:** Chinese travelers make decisions based on peer experiences (Xiaohongshu effect)
- **Intervention:** Partner with top 20 content creators; amplify visitor testimonials
- **Mechanism:** User-generated content is more trusted than brand messaging
- **Expected Uplift:** +35% awareness among target audience
- **Lab alignment:** Demonstrates social proof principle
