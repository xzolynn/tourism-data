## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Current Situation
- **Chinese visitors:** 8,200 in 2024 (31.7% of Fukui-Ishikawa market)
- **Average spend per Chinese visitor:** ¥265,000 (3.9% higher than Ishikawa)
- **Key insight:** Fukui attracts higher-spending Chinese visitors despite lower volume
- **Market opportunity:** Increase Chinese visitor volume by 15-20% through targeted nudge interventions

### Key Findings from Statistical Analysis
1. **Theme Preference is Non-Random (χ² = 19.92, p < 0.001)**
   - Scenic beauty dominates (24.6%)
   - Riku fandom drives 22.4% of engagement
   - Emotional resonance is critical for conversion

2. **Fandom is Quantifiable (t = 3.81, d = 2.54, p < 0.004)**
   - Riku-related events cause **27% visitor increase**
   - Effect size is **very large** (Cohen's d = 2.54)
   - This is the strongest predictor of visitor volume

3. **Emotional Content Outperforms Purely Informational Content**
   - Fandom & emotional themes: 0.88/1.0 emotional intensity
   - Logistics & practical content: 0.63/1.0 emotional intensity
   - **Recommendation:** Lead marketing with emotion, support with logistics

4. **Three Distinct Visitor Archetypes Identified**
   - Luxury Travelers (35%, prefer shopping)
   - Experience Seekers (39%, prefer accommodation)
   - Budget-Conscious Explorers (26%, cost-sensitive)
   - → Each needs tailored marketing approach

## COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS: US vs CHINESE VISITORS

### Chinese Visitors (Evidence-Based Profile)

**Motivation Structure:**
- Primary: Scenic beauty & fandom (47% combined)
- Secondary: Emotional/healing experience (21.7%)
- Tertiary: Practical (food, logistics) (31%)

**Spending Pattern (Japan-wide baseline):**
- Accommodation: 33.6% → HIGH priority
- Shopping: 29.5% → Focus on local souvenirs
- Food: 21.5% → Emphasize local cuisine
- Transport: 10.7% → Simplify booking
- Experience: 4.7% → Limited interest

**Behavior Patterns:**
- Social media-driven (Xiaohongshu, Douyin decision-making)
- Group/couple travel (romantic getaway positioning)
- High share-ability motivation (content creation)
- Medium stay duration (2-4 days optimal)

**Current Friction Points:**
1. **Transportation complexity** (JR pass selection, schedules)
2. **Language barriers** (information availability)
3. **Booking difficulty** (limited payment methods)
4. **Itinerary confusion** (too many options, no packaged tours)

### US Visitors (Hypothesis for Testing)

**Expected Motivation Structure:**
- Authentic local experience: 35-40%
- Natural/outdoor experiences: 25-30%
- Cultural heritage: 20-25%
- Novelty/unique destinations: 15-20%

**Hypothesized Spending Pattern:**
- Higher transportation budget (rental car, guided experiences)
- Flexible accommodation (wider range)
- Experience/activities: Likely higher than Asian markets
- Shopping: Lower priority

**Behavioral Characteristics:**
- Independent travel preference
- Blog/Google-driven planning (not social media)
- Longer stays (3-5 days typical)
- Lower social media sharing
- Diverse activity interests

**Friction Points (Hypothesis):**
1. Lack of authentic local experience opportunities
2. Limited English-language activity information
3. Rental car complexity in rural areas
4. Cultural experience scarcity

## STATISTICAL VALIDATION SUMMARY

### Analyses Completed (Lab Requirements)

✓ **Chi-Square Goodness-of-Fit Test** (Theme Distribution)
- Result: χ² = 19.92, p < 0.001 (Highly Significant)
- Interpretation: Chinese visitors have distinct, non-random preferences

✓ **ANOVA: Sentiment-Theme Correlation**
- Result: F = 19.39, p < 0.000001 (Highly Significant)
- Interpretation: Emotional content (fandom, emotional themes) outperforms informational content by 20-25%

✓ **K-Means Clustering: Visitor Segmentation**
- Result: 3 distinct archetypes identified with clear spending profiles
- Interpretation: One-size-fits-all marketing is suboptimal

✓ **Independent t-test: Event Impact Analysis**
- Result: t = 3.81, Cohen's d = 2.54, p < 0.004 (Very Large Effect)
- Interpretation: Riku events are the strongest predictor of visitor volume

✓ **Market Comparison Analysis: Fukui vs Ishikawa**
- Fukui: 31.7% market share, ¥265,000 avg spend
- Ishikawa: 68.3% market share, ¥255,000 avg spend
- Interpretation: Fukui is high-value but low-volume market
